CODE OF CONDUCT

While conducting the CTF event one must follow these set of rules:

MORALITIES TO BE FOLLOWED

1.Respect and Fair Play - Treat all participants, organizers, and volunteers with respect. Collaborate and compete in a spirit of fairness and good sportsmanship.

2.Ethical Behavior - Only target systems, challenges, and resources explicitly provided for the CTF event . Avoid attacking event infrastructure, other participants, or any unauthorized systems.

3. Inclusivity - The event is open to all students regardless of skill level. Harassment, discrimination, or any form of exclusionary behavior will not be tolerated.

4. Integrity - Do not engage in cheating, sharing flags, or using unauthorized tools/scripts designed to bypass the intended challenge solutions.

5. No Disruptive Actions - Refrain from attempting Denial of Service (DoS) attacks, spam, or any activity that may disrupt the event's infrastructure.

6. Follow the Instructions of Organizers - If there are issues with challenges or technical dificulties, contact the organizers immediately.

RULES OF THE EVENT

1. Team Composition - Teams can consist of up to 4 members. Solo participation is also allowed. Cross-college or external participation must be pre-approved by the organizers.

2. About the venue - the CTF is going to take place in the campus of Gyan Ganga Institute of technology and sciences . Participants have to carry ID card provided by the term . Entry without ID card is prohibited .

3. Event Timing - The CTF event will start at December 15 2024 and end at December 21 2024 . Submissions after the end time will not be counted.

4. Challenge Categories - The CTF will include various categories such as: Web Exploitation , Cryptography , Reverse Engineering , Binary exploitation, Forensics . Each challenge will have a designated point value based on its di culty.

5. Scoring System - Points are awarded based on the di culty of challenges. The team with the highest score at the end of the event wins. In the event of a tie, the team that solved the most challenges first will be ranked higher.

6. Flag Submission - Each challenge will have a flag in the format CTF .Flags must be submitted through the provided event platform. Sharing flags between teams is prohibited.

7. Use of Tools - Participants are encouraged to use their own tools, but automated tools that can disrupt the environment are restricted.

8. Communication - Teams are allowed to communicate internally but not with other teams. Any discussions about solving challenges should be confined to your own team.

LEGAL GUIDELINES AND RESTRICTION

1. Authorized Access Only - Participants are only allowed to interact with systems and services explicitly mentioned in the CTF challenges. Attempting to hack or interfere with systems outside the scope of the event is strictly prohibited and may result in disqualification and reporting to relevant authorities.

2. Data Privacy - No personal information should be shared, collected, or used during the event. Do not attempt to extract or misuse any sensitive data if found accidentally.

3. Compliance with Laws - All participants must comply with relevant local, state, and national laws. Any illegal activities during the event, such as unauthorized access to third-party systems or data, will be reported to the appropriate authorities.

4. No Use of Malicious Code - Participants must not upload, use, or distribute malware, viruses, or any malicious code that can harm systems, software, or data.

5. Disqualification Criteria - Violating any of the rules or the code of conduct will result in immediate disqualification. Severe cases of misconduct may lead to a ban from future events and/or reporting to authorities.

6. Liability Waiver - By participating in the event, you acknowledge that the organizers are not liable for any damages or losses that may occur as a result of your participation. Participants are responsible for their actions during the event and should practice safe and ethical hacking practices.

7. Contact Information and Support - If you encounter issues or need clarification during the event, contact the support team at CyberHive Support We encourage a collaborative and learning focused environment. Reach out if you need assistance understanding a concept or challenge.

By participating in this event, you agree to abide by the Code of Conduct, Rules, and Legal Guidelines outlined above. Let's work together to create a positive and educational experience for everyone!
